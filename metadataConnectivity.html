Identifier connectivity

Connectivity is the share of things that carry the identifier that could connect them — e.g. of the creators in a repository, how many have a name identifier (ORCID). The % is occurrence-based (identified occurrences ÷ all occurrences). The bar counts distinct entities (a person or affiliation that appears in many records is one entity): green = identified in every occurrence, yellow = identified in some (these are the quick wins — a known identifier to propagate), red = never identified. Click any bar to list them, worst connectivity first — the ones to add identifiers to.

Complete — identified in every occurrence Some — identified in some occurrences None — never identified
About: This tool measures identifier connectivity for a DataCite repository — distinct from completeness (whether an element is present). For each connector it reports, of the records that contain it, the share where every item is identified, some are, or none are; the per-bar percentage is the overall fraction of items that carry the identifier. Connectors: creators & contributors (name identifiers, affiliations, affiliation identifiers), funders (funder identifiers), and the publisher (publisher identifier). All computation runs in your browser against live DataCite data sent with affiliation=true&publisher=true — nothing is uploaded or stored.
