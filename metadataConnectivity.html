Identifier connectivity

Connectivity is the share of things that carry the identifier that could connect them — e.g. of the creators in a repository, how many have a name identifier (ORCID). The % is occurrence-based (identified occurrences ÷ all occurrences). The bar counts distinct entities (a person or affiliation that appears in many records is one entity): green = identified in every occurrence, yellow = identified in some (these are the quick wins — a known identifier to propagate), red = never identified. Click any bar to list them, color-coded to match the bars — quick wins first (a known identifier to propagate), then the ones still missing an identifier.

Complete — identified in every occurrence Some — identified in some occurrences None — never identified

Potential duplicatesadvisory

Likely-same people, organizations, and rights statements that appear under more than one spelling — to review and reconcile. This does not change the bars above. High confidence clusters share an identifier (the same ORCID/ROR under different names); review clusters are fuzzy string matches. “Can propagate” counts occurrences that could inherit a known identifier already present on another spelling. Acronyms vs full names (e.g. “MIT” vs the spelled-out name) only match when they share an identifier.

About: This tool measures identifier connectivity for a DataCite repository — distinct from completeness (whether an element is present). For each connector it reports, of the records that contain it, the share where every item is identified, some are, or none are; the per-bar percentage is the overall fraction of items that carry the identifier. Connectors: creators & contributors (name identifiers, affiliations, affiliation identifiers), funders (funder identifiers), the publisher (publisher identifier), and rights (rights URIs, rights identifiers). All computation runs in your browser against live DataCite data sent with affiliation=true&publisher=true — nothing is uploaded or stored. Learn more about connectivity ↗
